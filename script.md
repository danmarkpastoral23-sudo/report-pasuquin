## ⏱️ SLIDE 3 — WHY PASUQUIN? (~4–5 mins)

*[I-click next slide — Site Selection: Why Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte?]*

> "Ngayon, isa sa pinaka-importanteng desisyon sa pagdidisenyo ng wind farm
> ay yung site selection — kung saan mo itatayo.
> Hindi pwedeng basta-basta pumili ng lugar.
> Kailangan may matibay na engineering at technical na basehan.
> So bakit Pasuquin? May limang pangunahing dahilan."

> "Una — High Wind Power Density.
> Yung Pasuquin ay located along yung Northwest Luzon Wind Corridor,
> at highly exposed sa Northeast Monsoon — o yung tinatawag nating Amihan.
> Yung Amihan season ay tumatagal ng mga 6 buwan — October hanggang March —
> at consistently pumapasok yung malakas na hangin mula sa Luzon Strait papunta sa coast ng Pasuquin.
> Yung average wind speed dito ay 7 to 9 meters per second,
> na classified bilang IEC Wind Class 4 to 5.
> At yung capacity factor — o yung percentage ng oras na aktibo yung turbine sa full power —
> ay 35 to 45 percent.
> Para sa reference, yung average na coal plant ay may capacity factor na 70 to 85%,
> pero yung coal ay may fuel cost at emissions.
> Yung wind? Libre yung hangin. Kaya yung 38% capacity factor natin ay competitive na talaga."

> "Pangalawa — Strategic Coastal and Hilly Terrain.
> Yung Pasuquin ay may combination ng coastal plains at ridge areas —
> meaning dalawang magandang location tayo para sa turbines.
> Yung coastal area ay nakaka-capture ng sea breeze at Amihan winds directly,
> habang yung ridge areas naman ay mas mataas, kaya mas malakas pa yung hangin doon.
> So ma-ma-maximize natin yung energy output per turbine depende sa placement."

> "Pangatlo — Proximity to Existing Infrastructure.
> Malapit tayo sa Bangui at Burgos wind farms,
> na nangangahulugang established na yung NGCP transmission grid sa Ilocos Norte.
> Hindi na tayo kailangan mag-build ng bagong transmission line mula sa simula —
> connect na lang tayo sa existing infrastructure.
> Malaking tipid iyon sa construction cost."

> "Pang-apat — Eco-Tourism Potential.
> Alam nating lahat yung Bangui Wind Farm — iconic na tourist destination na to sa Ilocos Norte.
> Yung Pasuquin Wind Farm ay pwedeng sundin yung same formula —
> power plant at tourist landmark sabay-sabay.
> Public viewing deck, educational exhibits, local livelihood opportunities —
> hindi lang kuryente yung ibinibigay ng project to sa komunidad."

> "At panglima — Sustainability at Energy Security.
> To ay magrereduce ng fossil fuel reliance natin,
> magbibigay ng stable at predictable na energy supply sa Ilocos Norte,
> at susuporta sa layunin ng Pilipinas na maabot ang 35% RE share by 2030
> under yung Renewable Energy Act."

## ⏱️ SLIDE 4 — WIND-TO-GRID PROCESS FLOW (~8–10 mins)

*[I-click next slide — Section II-B: Wind-to-Grid Energy Flow]*

> "Ngayon, pag-usapan natin yung pinaka-importanteng parte —
> yung Process Flow Diagram ng Pasuquin Wind Farm.
> Ito yung journey ng energy, mula sa kinetic energy ng Amihan
> hanggang sa pagdeliver ng kuryente sa Luzon Grid.
> May pitong stages to."

---

### 🌀 Stage 1: Wind Resource — INPUT

> "Nagsisimula yung proseso sa wind resource —
> yung Amihan o Northeast Monsoon na pumapasok mula sa Luzon Strait
> sa approximately 8.5 meters per second.
> Ito yung ating raw material.
> Hindi tulad ng coal o gas, hindi ma-transport yung hangin —
> kaya kailangan talaga nating pumunta kung saan malakas yung hangin,
> at doon papasok si Pasuquin."

---

### 🌀 Stage 2: Rotor Blades — CAPTURE

> "Tinatamaan ng hangin yung 3 rotor blades —
> bawat isa ay 63.5 meters yung haba,
> gawa sa fiberglass-reinforced epoxy composite.
> Aerodynamically shaped yung mga blades para mag-create ng lift,
> na nagpapaikot sa rotor ng approximately 15 to 17 RPM.
> Yung pitch system — tatlong independent electric motors —
> yung nag-a-adjust ng blade angle para ma-optimize yung energy capture
> depende sa wind speed.
> Cut-in speed: 3 m/s. Cut-out speed: 25 m/s para sa safety."

---

### 🌀 Stage 3: Gearbox — Mechanical Conversion

> "Mula sa rotor, pumupunta yung energy sa loob ng nacelle.
> Yung low-speed shaft ay umiikot ng 15 to 20 RPM
> at konektado sa three-stage planetary gearbox
> na may gear ratio na 1 is to 90.
> Ini-step up nito yung rotation sa approximately 1,500 RPM
> sa high-speed shaft —
> yung speed na kailangan para i-drive yung generator sa 60 Hertz,
> yung standard grid frequency ng Pilipinas."

---

### 🌀 Stage 4: DFIG Generator — Electrical Conversion

> "Ini-drive ng high-speed shaft yung Doubly Fed Induction Generator, o DFIG.
> Kino-convert nito yung mechanical rotation
> sa 3-phase AC electricity na 690 volts.
> Yung partial-scale IGBT power converter yung nagse-stabilize ng output
> sa exactly 60 Hz kahit nag-vavary yung wind speed —
> critical to para sa grid compatibility."

---

### 🌀 Stage 5: Transformer 1 — Step-Up 690V → 33kV

> "Sa base ng bawat turbine, yung pad-mount transformer
> yung nag-i-step up ng voltage mula 690 volts papunta sa 33 kilovolts.
> Yung kuryente ay dumadaan sa underground XLPE cables —
> buried 1 meter deep — papunta sa central substation.
> Sa stage na to, approximately 5.70 megawatts net yung output ng bawat turbine."

---

### 🌀 Stage 6: Substation 33kV → 115kV + SCADA

> "Sa on-site substation, yung 120 MVA main transformer
> na may turns ratio na 3.48 is to 1
> yung nag-i-step up ng voltage mula 33 kV papunta sa 115 kV
> para sa long-distance transmission.
> Dito rin nandoon yung SCADA system —
> yung Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition —
> na nino-monitor yung lahat ng 20 turbines sa real time:
> power output, vibration, temperature, at fault alarms —
> lahat via OPC-UA protocol."

---

### 🌀 Stage 7: Luzon Grid — OUTPUT

> "Sa wakas, yung 115 kV electricity ay tinatransmit ng approximately
> 30 to 40 kilometers via overhead transmission lines
> papunta sa NGCP grid metering point sa Laoag.
> Pagkatapos ng lahat ng electrical losses —
> cables, transformers, at transmission line —
> yung net output per turbine ay approximately 5.58 megawatts.
> Para sa 20 turbines, iyon ay total annual energy yield
> na approximately 309,580 megawatt-hours per year —
> sapat para mapagsilbihan yung mga 77,400 Filipino households."

## ⏱️ SLIDE 6 — DESIGN CONSTRAINTS (~5–6 mins)

*[I-click next slide — Design Constraints: Engineering Basis & Limitations]*

> "Ngayon, pag-usapan natin yung mga engineering constraints na ginamit ko
> sa pagdidisenyo ng wind farm to.
> May anim na pangunahing constraints."

---

### 🔴 C1: Betz Limit

> "Una — yung Betz Limit.
> To yung pinaka-fundamental na physical constraint sa wind energy.
> Proven ni Albert Betz noong 1919 na
> walang turbine yung makakaextract ng higit sa 59.3% ng kinetic energy ng hangin.
> Gamit yung Wind Power Density equation —
> P equals one-half, times air density, times swept area, times wind speed cubed —
> nakalkula ko na sa 8.5 m/s at 130-meter rotor diameter,
> yung available power ay approximately 27.7 megawatts.
> After applying yung Betz limit at drivetrain losses,
> yung practical rated output ay 5 megawatts per turbine."

---

### 🔴 C2: Wake Effect Spacing

> "Pangalawa — yung Wake Effect Spacing.
> Kapag nag-extract ng energy yung isang turbine mula sa hangin,
> nagche-create ito ng zone ng reduced wind speed at increased turbulence sa likod nito.
> Para mabawasan to, yung turbines ay dapat nakalagay nang
> 7 rotor diameters apart sa downwind direction — mga 910 meters —
> at 4 rotor diameters crosswind — mga 520 meters.
> Yung spacing constraint na to yung directly nagdetermina
> ng total land area requirement na approximately 9.46 square kilometers."

---

### 🔴 C3: Wind Shear — Hub Height

> "Pangatlo — yung Wind Shear constraint.
> Yung wind speed ay tumataas habang tumataas yung altitude,
> ayon sa Power Law equation.
> Sa ground level, yung wind speed ay around 6.5 m/s.
> Sa 100 meters hub height, tumataas ito sa 8.19 m/s — 26% na pagtaas.
> Dahil yung wind power ay proportional sa velocity cubed,
> ito ay nangangahulugang roughly twice yung power kumpara sa ground level.
> Kaya justified yung cost ng 90 to 110-meter towers."

---

### 🔴 C4: Safety Setbacks

> "Pang-apat — yung Safety Setbacks.
> Yung mga turbine ay dapat hindi bababa sa 500 meters
> mula sa mga tirahan para sa noise compliance — below 45 decibels —
> at hindi bababa sa 200 meters mula sa coastline
> bilang storm surge buffer.
> Hindi to negotiable — safety at regulatory approval requirement to."

---

### 🔴 C5: Grid Voltage — 33kV

> "Panlima — yung electrical constraint.
> Yung collection voltage ay itinakda sa 33 kilovolts —
> industry standard to para sa wind farms up to 200 megawatts.
> Yung step-up transformer ratio ay 3.48 to 1 —
> mula 33 kV papunta sa 115 kV —
> para mag-match sa existing NGCP Ilocos Norte grid standard."

---

### 🔴 C6: Foundation Loads

> "Pang-anim — yung Foundation Loads constraint.
> Yung bawat turbine base ay kailangang withstand ng overturning moment
> na 115,000 kilonewton-meters sa tower base.
> Nangangailangan to ng octagonal reinforced concrete mat foundation,
> 21 meters yung lapad, na may 144 anchor bolts.
> Yung safety factor laban sa overturning ay 2.30 —
> well above yung required minimum na 1.5.
> So ligtas tayo dito."
